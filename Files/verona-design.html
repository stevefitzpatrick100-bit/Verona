Verona · Confidential · April 2026

A place to come back to.

A working document for one builder. Not a PRD. Not a spec. A spine — written so the version of me who returns next week remembers why this shape and not some other shape.

i.

The one paragraph

settled —

If I cannot write this paragraph cleanly on a given day, that is a signal.

Verona is a matchmaker, not a dating app. An AI named Angelica gets to know one person — slowly, warmly, over weeks and months — through a conversation that feels less like a form and more like a friend who is paying quiet attention. From that knowing she builds a portrait, finds compatible matches, and orchestrates good evenings with people who want to share them. The promise is not a perfect partner on the first try; it is worth your Tuesday evening. The product succeeds when someone stops needing it.

ii.

The founding promise

settled —

Structural commitments. If a feature drifts against one of these, it is the wrong feature.

Angelica works for the person, not the platform.
No engagement metrics. No reason to keep them searching. The only success that counts is users who stopped needing us.
Trust is earned, never assumed.
Angelica's licence to interpret, push back, or speak plainly is unlocked by user behaviour, not by session count or feature gating. She holds the read silently until trust says otherwise.
No ads, no profiles, no ranking.
Built into the architecture, not the marketing. Verona is not-for-profit in structure — that's what makes the trust rational rather than naive.
Worth your Tuesday evening.
The Stage 4 promise. Not "your perfect partner" — that frame poisons every date. A compatible person, free the same evening, both wanting the same specific thing. The weight comes off. The surprise has room to happen.
iii.

The model

settled —

Stages × Levels × Phases. The one place these three are reconciled.

Angelica's behaviour at any moment is a composition of three things: which stage the user is in (what the conversation is for), which level the relationship has reached (how she is licensed to speak), and which phase she is in within that stage (the within-stage rhythm). These compose. They do not collapse into each other.

Stage What the conversation is for A property of the relationship. Five chapters the user lives through. Mostly forward, but revisitable.
Level How Angelica is licensed to speak A property of the relationship. Three levels of earned intimacy. Monotonic — only moves up.
Phase The within-stage rhythm Trust → Hypothesis → Diffusion. Mostly relevant inside Stages 1–3.

The five stages

Stage 1 Building Trust Soft → Stage 2
A warm stranger paying close attention. Angelica is making the relationship pleasant and specific enough to come back to. She holds a private read but says nothing of it. Ends when the user volunteers something they weren't directly asked for.
Stage 2 Knowing Yourself Gated → Stage 3
The portrait builds. The Michelangelo moment lives here — what the user has always been, made legible. The essential truth gets spoken, framed as their insight not Angelica's diagnosis. Ends when the portrait is representable to another agent.
Stage 3 What Are You Looking For Gated → Stage 4
Turning outward. The partner image and relationship image crystallise. Not a checklist — a brief that the user owns and Angelica understands the same way. Ends when the partner image is matchable.
Stage 4 Good Evenings Soft → Stage 5
Three-factor matching: compatibility, shared availability, shared interest in a specific thing. Angelica orchestrates evenings worth having. Runs on frequency. Not auditioning spouses.
Stage 5 Something More → Disappearance
Runs on emergence, not optimisation. Angelica steps back, witnesses, tells the truth as she sees it, trusts the user to live it. Ends when a real relationship has formed and she gracefully goes quiet.

The three levels

Level 1 Warm Stranger Unlocks → user volunteers something exposed
Pleasant. Curious. Light. One question at a time, follows energy. Holds the read entirely silently — nothing she says contradicts the user's account of themselves.
Level 2 Someone Who Knows Them Unlocks → user accepts an observation
Familiar. Remembers, synthesises, begins to notice aloud. Offers small reflections framed with humility, always with an exit.
Level 3 Trusted Advisor Held; never lost
Direct, economical, warm without preamble. Says what she sees. The ceremony drops away because trust has made it unnecessary. The level sets the ceiling; the register sets the floor.

How they compose

Stage × Level × Phase × Substrate state, plus the live personality parameters, produces the system prompt at any given moment. The substrate never stops — portrait, partner image, relationship image, brief — all updating continuously regardless of which stage the user is in. Stages are the chapters the user lives through; the substrate work is what makes the chapters mean anything.

iv.

The system, in three loops

current best guess —

An architectural decision still in motion. Today these three loops are partly fused. The next architectural move is to separate them.

The right architecture has three independent loops, each on its own cadence, with strict boundaries about what feeds what. Loop 2 makes Angelica work. Loop 3 tells you whether it's working. They must not be the same call.

Loop 1
Angelica
Every turn
The conversation. Composes the system prompt from base character + Level rules + Stage rules + portrait context + personality parameters, produces the next message.ReadsThe composed prompt; recent messages.WritesThe reply.
Loop 2
Portrait & Personality
Every turn, after Loop 1
What Angelica needs to know to be Angelica next turn. Updates the portrait, fragments, hypotheses, silences, key moments, territory, essential truth, partner image, personality parameters.ReadsThe latest exchange + current substrate.WritesSubstrate updates. Internal to Angelica's operation.
Loop 3
CQ Observer
Session-level, separate
Independent judgement on the conversation itself. The 15 CQ dimensions. Read-only with respect to Angelica.ReadsThe full transcript, against a versioned rubric.WritesCQ measurements, observer notes. Re-runnable. Swappable. Never feeds back into Angelica's prompt.

The test of correct separation: I should be able to delete Loop 3 entirely and Angelica still functions identically. I should be able to rerun Loop 3 over old sessions with a new rubric and get fresh judgements without touching the conversation data. Today neither is true.

vi.

Open questions

open —

The questions I keep coming back to. Each has a current best guess, kept honest about its uncertainty.

How should CQ be measured — per turn, per session, or both?
Canonical CQ is a session-close pass over the full transcript, with a versioned rubric. A lighter per-turn read may be useful as a trend signal, but it is not the source of truth.
Do per-turn personality parameters need to be captured before scaling users?
Yes. Without them I cannot tell whether Angelica's voice dialled correctly to the moment, and I cannot debug when it didn't. Foundational, not v2.
How does Stage 2 → Stage 3 actually gate? Who decides the portrait is representable?
Mechanical gates (dimension count, confidence thresholds) plus a representability check — can Angelica produce a coherent paragraph describing this person to another agent? If she balks, the gate isn't passed.
When should Stage 5 silence trigger? How does Angelica know to step back?
A signal in the conversation, not a metric. Words like seeing someone, mentions of meeting their friends, plans more than two weeks out. The simultaneous-cessation-of-searching idea from the original conversation.
What is the smallest viable matching system for Stage 4?
Pre-agent-to-agent: a manual operator surface in the admin where I can see two compatible portraits side by side and orchestrate a single introduction. Use this to learn what the agent eventually needs to do.
vii.

Out of scope (with reasons)

decided —

What I'm explicitly not building, and why. The hardest section to write and the most useful — without it, every old idea comes back as if for the first time.

No engagement metrics, retention curves, or DAU dashboards. Tracking these turns the product into something that wants users to stay. Verona wants users to leave, gracefully. Reading sessions is the metric.

No prompt-tuning UI in the admin. The friction of editing prompts in code is a feature. It makes me slow down. I need to read sessions before changing prompts; a fast UI would invite the opposite.

No user-facing portrait. The user does not see what Angelica is building about them. The portrait is the substrate, not the product. Showing it would make users perform for it, and the whole edifice depends on them not performing.

No A/B testing of system prompts. The unit of evaluation is the relationship over months, not the message over minutes. A/B testing optimises for short-loop signals, which is exactly the failure mode of every other dating product.

No human in the matching loop, ever. Pure AI. This is the trust. A human reviewer would compromise the founding promise — and the user would always wonder which conversation was being read by whom.

No multi-operator admin. Single-operator until the design has stopped moving. Roles, permissions, audit logs are v3 or later, post-launch.
